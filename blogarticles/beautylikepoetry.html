Of being born in the right place

I have never been able to understand why my brother disliked men wearing nail polish or skirts, and why my mother thought only women of a certain figure could wear revealing or form-defining dresses. When I saw a person wearing nail polish, I was in awe, for all my attempts of painting my nails has resulted in a hideous bloch of colour on the tip of all fingers, a few on my palm and on the worst of occasions clothing as well. Of course, learning to draw within lines is a skill we were ought to learn in kindergarten, but painting within the rims of my nail beds was far beyond my capability it seemed. When I saw a person with mesmerizing eyeshadow, I tried to memorize and copy it later, or a dress that I found appealing, I thought of how wonderful having one of my own might be. I understood quite the principle that some things for the sake of hair colour or complexion would emphasize different feature, but else a dress that I found beautiful would not change into a dress that I find hideous by the face of the one in it. The idea that a dress that I loved, I would love less depending on the wearer never occurred to me, for the simple thought seems so unreasonable. Would it mean that if I owned a dress and lent it to two people, it would turn more pretty with one, and more ugly with the other? Would not paint on the nail, be of the same pretty colour and applied of the same admirable skill, wether painted on the hand of a man or of a woman?
